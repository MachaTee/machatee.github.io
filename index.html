Student Voice
Practitioners Network

The Student Voice Practitioners’ Network brings together Higher Education professionals with an interest in student feedback, working with students and widening participation. We offer a platform for knowledge sharing, support and participation in termly meetings and events designed to enhance the student voice, share great practice and work together collaboratively.

About the Network

We regularly look at a wide range of topics and emerging practices relating to student voice, engagement, and partnership working across higher education. Including:

Reducing Survey Fatigue
Engaging Staff as well as Students
Creative Methods of Capturing Student Feedback
Closing the Loop
Use of AI to Analyse Feedback


We believe that the student voice is an essential part of a student’s experience at university, so we created the Student Voice Practitioners’ Network where colleagues from across the sector can come together to gain support and knowledge in an informal, dynamic and agile way.

The network was founded by colleagues at the University of Sussex and the University of East London and has now grown to include around 200 representatives from over 90 different Higher Education organisations.

It is a space to connect with professional peers to discuss common challenges, showcase good practice, troubleshoot and work in partnership with one another. Members range from those working in student experience and widening participation, to those focused on quality and evaluation.

The group aims to meet four times a year, primarily online with an in-person conference in May, with agendas being suggested by members in a collaborative approach.

We've covered topics including:

Student Voice Principles

Exploring the core principles that underpin meaningful student voice, including representation, partnership, inclusivity and impact. This is a space to reflect on what student voice looks like in practice and how we embed these values across our work.

Methods to engage Student Voice

A chance to share and discover effective approaches for engaging students, from traditional feedback mechanisms to more creative and inclusive methods. We swap ideas, tools and experiences to help reach a diverse range of student voices.

Responding to NSS results

We discuss how to interpret and respond to NSS outcomes in a meaningful way. This includes sharing strategies for closing the feedback loop, communicating with students, and turning insights into tangible improvements.

The role of Student Voice in a changing landscape

An opportunity to reflect on how student voice is evolving in response to sector changes. We consider emerging challenges, opportunities, and how practitioners can adapt their approaches to ensure student voice remains impactful and relevant.

Student Voice Conference

Every May we host a free student voice conference for practitioners working in student voice.

We have previously hosted at the University of Sussex, Kings College London and London South Bank.
